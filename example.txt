## ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## ETCHING examples for DEMO

## Note:
## Even if you don't have normal sequencing data, ETCHING can predict somatic SVs with pretty high recall
## and precision rates by using PGK2 (http://big.hanyang.ac.kr/ETCHING/). Its performance without normal data
## is comparable with other state-of-art somatic SV callers requiring normal data. In the following example,
## replace demo_PGK with PGK2 for real data. When you download PGK2, you will have PGK2.kmc_pre and
## PGK2.kmc_suf. Then, type like "-f PGK2" (or"-f /path/to/PGK2").

## Requirement for running ETCHING:
## samtools (>=1.13) and bwa

## ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## Get demo

wget http://big.hanyang.ac.kr/ETCHING/DEMO.tar.gz
tar zxvf DEMO.tar.gz
cd DEMO

## or

wget http://big.hanyang.ac.kr/ETCHING/tiny_DEMO.tar.gz
tar zxvf tiny_DEMO.tar.gz
cd tiny_DEMO

## ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## FASTQ input with matched normal

etching -1 tumor_1.fq -2 tumor_2.fq -1c normal_1.fq -2c normal_2.fq \
-g small_genome.fa -a small_genome.gtf -f demo_PGK -o example_1 -t 8

## ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## Somatic SV prediction without matched normal sequencing data

etching -1 tumor_1.fq -2 tumor_2.fq \
-g small_genome.fa -a small_genome.gtf -f demo_PGK -o example_2 -t 8

## ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## BAM input

etching -b tumor.sort.bam -bc normal.sort.bam \
-g small_genome.fa -a small_genome.gtf -f demo_PGK -o example_3 -t 8

## ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## CRAM input

etching -b tumor.sort.cram -bc normal.sort.cram \
-g small_genome.fa -a small_genome.gtf -f demo_PGK -o example_4 -t 8
